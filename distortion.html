Distortion, also known as overdrive, is an effect that boosts the incoming signal (gain) which causes it to distort and break up, resulting it a gritty or fuzzy sound. This is a very common effect, especially on guitar, and is the cornerstone of the sound of rock and its descendants like metal and punk.

The effect works by first increasing the gain of the input signal, i.e. increasing its peak-to-peak voltage. That signal is then passed to a clipping stage, which cuts the voltage off at some maximum value. The higher the gain, the more of the signal is clipped. This clipping results in signal information being lost above the threshold, and hard edges where it is cut off. The now distorted signal passes through some filtering and output volume adjustment, depending on the design of the pedal.
